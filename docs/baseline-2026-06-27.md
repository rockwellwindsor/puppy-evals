## What these numbers mean

**Retrieval at 86.7% (both puppies)** — The same retrieval pipeline serves both puppies, so identical scores are expected. 86.7% means roughly 4 out of every 30 expected chunk sections didn't come back. The failing questions haven't been identified yet — that requires the dashboard detail view (Step 17).

**Grounding: Gus 4.20, Mitch 2.93** — The most significant gap in the baseline. Gus's more measured answers stay close to the retrieved context. Mitch's enthusiastic style appears to be causing embellishment beyond what the chunks support — or the grounding judge is penalising informal phrasing even when the facts are correct. Calibration (Step 13) will distinguish between these two explanations.

**Persona: Gus 3.53, Mitch 3.80** — Mitch's enthusiastic voice is easier for GPT-3.5 to maintain consistently than Gus's more nuanced sophistication. Neither score is strong. Both are expected to improve once Gus and Mitch's system prompts are expanded (planned as Experiment 3).

---

## Known limitations at this baseline

- **Judges are not yet calibrated.** Scores reflect what Haiku thinks, not yet validated against human judgment. Calibration (Step 13) happens next and may shift scores meaningfully.
- **Golden set is first-draft.** Some questions may have incorrect expected chunk sections or ambiguous example answers. Errors will surface during calibration and dashboard review.
- **Personas are thin.** The Windsor site's system prompts for Gus and Mitch are minimal. Persona scores are expected to improve significantly once those are expanded.
- **No cost tracking yet.** The `cost_usd` column in `eval_results` is not populated — the chatbot endpoint doesn't return cost data and Haiku costs weren't tracked per-call in this run.

---

## What to improve first

1. **Calibrate the judges** — hand-score 10 outputs and compare to judge scores. If Mitch's grounding scores look wrong to human eyes, the judge prompt needs tuning before the number means anything.
2. **Investigate Mitch's grounding** — after calibration, identify which specific questions scored lowest and why.
3. **Expand the personas** — Experiment 3 will tweak the system prompts and re-run. The delta from this baseline is the signal.
